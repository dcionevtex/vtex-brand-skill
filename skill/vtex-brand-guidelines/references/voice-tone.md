# VTEX Brand Voice and Tone

Source: [brand.vtex.com](https://brand.vtex.com/)

---

## Core Philosophy

**"It is never about us."**

The customer is the protagonist. VTEX is the guide. Every piece of content should be framed around what the audience experiences, achieves, or overcomes — not around VTEX's capabilities or history.

---

## The Three Personality Dimensions

These traits must always be in balance. No single trait dominates.

| Trait | Positive expression | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Bold** | Fresh, transformative, direct, confident | Immature, aggressive, provocative for its own sake |
| **Trustworthy** | Knowledgeable, technically authoritative, credible | Pretentious, jargon-heavy, cold |
| **Optimistic** | Confident about outcomes, forward-looking, solutions-oriented | Naive, unrealistic, marketing-speak |

If a piece of copy feels too salesy → it's Bold without Trustworthy.  
If it feels too academic → it's Trustworthy without Bold or Optimistic.  
If it feels like fluff → it's Optimistic without substance.

---

## Communication Principles

### 1. Audience-first framing

Open with the customer's reality: their challenge, their goal, their context.

**Wrong:**
> "VTEX enables enterprises to achieve faster time-to-market through our composable commerce platform."

**Right:**
> "Uploading hundreds of thousands of SKUs used to take several hours of your day. Now it doesn't."

### 2. Simple and direct

Remove jargon or define it immediately. Every sentence should be actionable or insightful.

**Wrong:**
> "Our headless, API-first, cloud-native SaaS platform leverages microservices architecture to deliver omnichannel synergies."

**Right:**
> "One platform for your store, app, marketplace, and B2B portal — all running from the same backend."

### 3. Guide, not hero

VTEX enables the customer to succeed. The customer succeeds. VTEX made it possible.

**Wrong:**
> "VTEX solved the scalability problem for Grupo Boticário."

**Right:**
> "Grupo Boticário scaled to 30 million daily requests — without changing their commerce backend."

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Slide deck headline | Bold, declarative, short — max 8 words |
| Executive summary | Confident, precise, no filler |
| Case study | Factual, customer-voice, outcome-focused |
| Technical documentation | Direct, instructional, no marketing |
| Campaign copy | Punchy, emotional, Bold-dominant |
| B2B proposal | Trustworthy-dominant, evidence-backed, structured |
| Event/social | Optimistic, energetic, human |

---

## Headline Patterns

Use these structural patterns for slide headlines and document titles:

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Problem → solved | "Black Friday at scale. Zero downtime." |
| Customer outcome | "From 3 platforms to 1. Revenue up 40%." |
| Direct benefit | "Launch in weeks, not months." |
| Bold claim + qualifier | "The last platform you'll need to migrate to." |
| Question that frames content | "What does composable actually cost?" |

---

## Words to Use

- Scale, launch, grow, expand, connect
- Commerce, operations, catalog, orders
- Composable, headless (always define it)
- Fast, reliable, flexible
- Merchants, operators, teams (instead of "customers" in B2B)

## Words to Avoid

- "Revolutionize," "disrupt," "game-changing"
- "End-to-end solution" (vague)
- "Best-in-class," "world-class" (unsubstantiated)
- "Leverage" as a verb (corporate filler)
- "Seamless" without definition
- "Empower" without context

---

## Language for Different Audiences

| Audience | Emphasis |
|---|---|
| CTO / Technical | Architecture, API, reliability, speed, ops control |
| CMO / Business | Time-to-market, conversion, flexibility, cost of ownership |
| Operations / IT | Integration effort, migration risk, support, stability |
| C-level / Executive | Market position, total cost, speed, competitive advantage |

---

## Copy Review Checklist

Before finalizing any document or slide:

- [ ] Does the opening sentence reference the audience, not VTEX?
- [ ] Are all bold claims backed by data or a customer example?
- [ ] Is the language free of undefined jargon?
- [ ] Does the narrative position the customer as the one who achieves the outcome?
- [ ] Is the tone balanced between Bold, Trustworthy, and Optimistic?
- [ ] Are headlines under 10 words and declarative?
- [ ] Is there zero use of "seamless," "leverage," or "game-changing"?
